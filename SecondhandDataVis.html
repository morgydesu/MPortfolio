Product Strategy & Market Intelligence

The Resale Revolution

Building an interactive data product to visualize the correlation between social media trends and the $200B+ secondhand fashion market.

Role

Product Lead

Duration

4 Months

Impact

9 User Iterations

Tech Stack

JS (Plotly), Figma

01. Market Opportunity

While the secondhand market is growing 11x faster than traditional retail, many lack a clear understanding of why. Most reports focus on static numbers, missing the cultural causation behind the data.

The Goal: To develop an interactive "Data-Article" that visualizes the direct correlation between cultural catalysts (like Macklemore or #ThriftTok) and consumer behavior shifts.

02. Discovery & Data Parsing

Data Acquisition

Sourced and parsed secondary data from Google Trends and NCBI. I identified key spikes in "thrifting" interest and cross-referenced them with historical events.

The Strategic Pivot

Initial research was too broad. Following "Stakeholder Feedback" (Tutor), I pivoted to focus on the unethical impact of fast fashion (ASOS/AI) to create a more compelling problem-solution narrative.

03. Product Implementation

I built a custom interactive engine using JavaScript (Plotly.js) to transform static data into dynamic modules. This allows users to engage with the data (Retail vs. Resale) through active filtering and responsive charts.

Technical Feasibility

"How to convey the narrative?"

Mapped variables in Figma before coding to ensure the visual hierarchy emphasized the 'Gender Gap' and 'COVID-19 surge' data points.

Omni-Channel UI

"Tech-savvy audience"

Designed for tech-savvy, budget-conscious users, prioritizing a clean "Vintage-Sustainable" aesthetic that reduced bounce rates during testing.

05. Key Takeaways

Data Synthesis & Analysis

Mastered the process of Data Acquisition—sourcing raw datasets from Google Trends and NCBI, then using Excel to parse and identify non-trivial correlations between social media spikes and market shifts.

Strategic Storytelling

Translated complex technical analysis into a captivating narrative. I focused on "Simple Terms" to ensure that non-technical stakeholders could immediately grasp the impact of #ThriftTok on the $200B resale economy.

Organic Growth Strategy

Identified that high-traffic cultural touchstones (like Macklemore or COVID-19) serve as the best user entry points. By leading with these "hooks," I increased user engagement with the deeper economic data.

Iterative Validation

Used a Double-Diamond approach: moving from raw data exploration to user-tested prototypes. Each iteration focused on refining the "Data-to-Insight" pipeline based on direct tester feedback.
