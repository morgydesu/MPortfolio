01. Problem Landscape

THE "WHY"

The User Friction

Field staff were disengaged from corporate communications. Research revealed that portals were hard to find, submission forms were intimidating, and "confirmation anxiety" led to duplicate inquiries.

The Technical Debt

The FX team spent hours manually converting PDFs into newsletters. These newsletters frequently "broke" in legacy Outlook clients, resulting in unprofessional branding and unreadable content.

02. Product Strategy

Unified Access

Consolidate disparate entry points into a single "Engagement Portal" embedded directly where staff already reviews (eg. Tech Plus, Communication Collection, etc).

Automation-First Logic

Move away from manual design. Use Power Automate to bridge the gap between form data and final HTML rendering.

Validation & Reassurance

Implement a "Preview Mode" and automated approval loops to build trust and eliminate manual verification steps.

03. Visual Transformation

EVOLUTION

The primary challenge was technical debt. The legacy state relied on manual PDF conversions that broke in Outlook, while the new engineered state uses a portal-driven HTML engine.

Legacy

Fragmented formatting and manual PDF attachments.

Engineered

Automated, responsive HTML with 100% brand fidelity.

04. Implementation

THE "HOW"

Phase 1: Discovery (Double Diamond)

Conducted primary user sessions to map the "Engagement Journey." Identified that 70% of friction was due to unclear UX copy of the Engagement Portal.

Phase 2: Technical Iteration

Built the Power Automate flow and the hybrid HTML/VML template. Pushed adjustments in October to fix deep-link issues between SOW and the Engagement portal.

Phase 3: Super User Launch

Deployed to internal "FX Super Users" in November. Monitored data integrity, user process acceptance, and automation flow.

05. Lifecycle Strategy

TIMELINE

Managing a 10-month timeline required balancing immediate "Super User" needs with the long-term marketing vision. I successfully decoupled technical stability from market-wide adoption.

May – Oct

Discovery & Adjustments

06. Technical UX Fidelity

"Technical constraints are just product requirements in disguise."

By re-engineering the email engine into a hybrid VML/HTML structure, I ensured 100% brand fidelity and layout stability across different email clients—eliminating the "broken" formatting issues that previously plagued field communications.

07. Product Takeaways

Technical Empathy

Bridging the gap between design desire and legacy Outlook reality. Success was found by engineering for legacy constraints without sacrificing brand prestige.

Outcome vs. Output

The outcome was hours of human capacity returned to the FX team. Focusing on the "Hours Saved" KPI kept stakeholders aligned during timeline shifts.

Phased De-risking

Separating the Super User launch from the GTM was vital. It allowed us to fix edge cases in a controlled environment before wide-scale adoption.

Iterative Research

Research isn't a one-time step. User feedback must be a continuous loop throughout the development lifecycle to ensure product-market fit.
